**Explore A0: Living and Non-Living Things**

We see many things in our world every day. All these things are living or non-living. How are they different?

**Living Things**
A living thing can grow and change. A plant is a living thing. An animal is a living thing. You are a living thing! Living things need air, food, and water. People and animals can move. They can get food and water from different places. Plants cannot move by themselves. They make their food from sunlight, water, and air.

**Questions:**
- What living things do you see every day?

**Non-living Things**

A non-living thing does not need air, food, or water. A toy is a non-living thing. A non-living thing does not grow, but it can change. Look at the shoes. How are they different?

A ball can move. Is it a living thing? No, it isn't. It cannot move by itself. It does not grow. Look around you. What non-living things do you see?

All living and non-living things in our world are important. We should take care of them.

**Grandma Lihos and the Bamboo Steamer**

**Dialogue:**
- Grandma Lihos: "I use a bamboo steamer to make boozl. How can we make a steamer?"

**Vocabulary:**
- Bamboo: A type of tall grass that can be used to make various items.
- Steamer: A cooking device that uses steam to cook food.
- Boozl: A dish that is made using a bamboo steamer.

**Process of Making a Bamboo Steamer:**
1. Bamboo can grow very tall.
2. People cut down the bamboo.
3. People cut the bamboo into pieces.
4. People turn the bamboo pieces into a steamer.

**Concept:**
- Bamboo begins as a living thing. When people make a steamer with the bamboo pieces, the bamboo becomes a non-living thing.

**Exercises:**
1. Discuss with a partner how bamboo is used in your culture.
2. List three items that can be made from bamboo.
3. Draw a picture of a bamboo steamer and label its parts.
